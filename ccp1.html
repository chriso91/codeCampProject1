A Time Line of Tie-Dye

  • Involves folding, twisting, pleating or crumping fabric and binding it with rubber bands followed by the application of dyes.
  • Uses bright, saturated primary colors and bold patterns.
  • The term tie-dye was coined in the 60s but was referred to as tie and die in text in the 40s.
  • Hit it's peak popularity in the 60s and 70s.
  • The earliest surviving examples date from 500 to 810 in Peru.
  • Seen in cultures from China, Japan, Indonesia, Thailand and Africa.
  • It is believed that African tie-dye inspired it's use in hippie fashion.
  • Introduced to America in the early 1900s.
